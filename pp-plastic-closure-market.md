<p><p>The PP plastic closure market primarily utilizes two molding techniques: compression molding and injection molding. Compression molding involves shaping the material by applying heat and pressure, resulting in durable closures with precise dimensions; it's commonly used for larger, thicker designs. Injection molding, on the other hand, injects molten polypropylene into molds, allowing for intricate shapes and faster production rates. Both methods cater to diverse packaging needs, ensuring strong, lightweight, and versatile closure solutions for various industries.</p></p>
