<p><p>The medium-density fiberboard (MDF) market is categorized into two primary types: monolayer and multi-storey. Monolayer MDF consists of a single-layer construction, offering versatility and uniformity, making it suitable for various applications in furniture and cabinetry. In contrast, multi-storey MDF features multiple layers, enhancing structural stability and durability, often used in heavy-duty applications like flooring and wall panels. Both types cater to specific market needs, driven by design preferences and performance requirements across residential and commercial sectors.</p></p>
