<p><ul><li>Active Chlorine Acrylate Rubber</li><li>Epoxy Acrylate Rubber</li><li>Dienes Acrylate Rubber</li><li>Carboxyl Type Acrylate Rubber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyacrylate elastomers are synthetic rubbers known for their heat, oil, and ozone resistance. **Active Chlorine Acrylate Rubber** contains chlorine groups, enhancing flame retardancy and adhesion. **Epoxy Acrylate Rubber** incorporates epoxy groups, improving chemical resistance and mechanical properties. **Dienes Acrylate Rubber** includes diene monomers, offering better flexibility and low-temperature performance. **Carboxyl Type Acrylate Rubber** features carboxyl groups, enhancing crosslinking and bonding strength. Each type caters to specific industrial needs, such as automotive seals, gaskets, and hoses, balancing durability, chemical resistance, and thermal stability.</p></p>
