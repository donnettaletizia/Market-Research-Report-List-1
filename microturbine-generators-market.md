<p><p>Microturbine generators are compact power generation systems that vary primarily in their design for efficiency and application. Unrecuperated microturbines operate without a heat recovery mechanism, leading to lower efficiencies but simpler designs and reduced costs, making them suitable for smaller or remote power needs. In contrast, recuperated microturbines incorporate a heat exchanger to recycle exhaust heat, enhancing overall efficiency and making them ideal for combined heat and power (CHP) applications, industrial processes, and larger energy demands.</p></p>
