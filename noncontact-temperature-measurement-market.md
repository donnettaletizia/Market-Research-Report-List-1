<p><p>The noncontact temperature measurement market encompasses methods that gauge temperature without direct contact with the object. Infrared temperature measurement utilizes infrared sensors to detect thermal radiation, allowing quick and accurate readings from a distance, ideal for moving objects or hazardous environments. Optical fiber temperature measurement employs fiber optic technology to sense temperature changes along the fiber, offering high precision and resistance to electromagnetic interference. Both methods find applications across industries like manufacturing, healthcare, and aerospace for effective monitoring and control.</p></p>
