<p><ul><li>Oxide Ceramics</li><li>Non-Oxide Ceramics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The structural ceramics market is categorized into oxide and non-oxide ceramics. Oxide ceramics, primarily composed of aluminum oxide, zirconium oxide, and silicon dioxide, are known for their high thermal stability and resistance to corrosion, making them suitable for applications like cutting tools and biomedical devices. Non-oxide ceramics, including carbides, nitrides, and borides, offer superior hardness and wear resistance, often used in high-performance applications such as aerospace, automotive, and electronics. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, driving innovation in materials technology.</p></p>
