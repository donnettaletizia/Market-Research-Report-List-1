<p><p>Engineering plastics in the automotive market are categorized into thermosetting and thermoplastic types. Thermosetting plastics, which harden permanently upon heating, provide excellent heat resistance and structural integrity, making them suitable for components like electrical housings and engine covers. In contrast, thermoplastics can be reshaped upon heating, offering flexibility and ease of processing. They are widely used in parts like dashboards and lightweight structural components, contributing to reduced vehicle weight and improved fuel efficiency. Each type plays a crucial role in automotive applications.</p></p>
